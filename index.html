Travel Notes & Tips
  • Book Forbidden City tickets up to 7 days in advance — they sell out fast. Use the official WeChat mini-program.
  • Trains within China: book ~1 month ahead. Long-distance segments can also be replaced by domestic flights, often same price — book 50 days ahead for best deals.
  • Golden Week (Oct 1–8): pre-book timed tickets online for cable cars, the Li River cruise, and key attractions to skip queues on the day — much easier on everyone, especially with Dad along.
  • Subway in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen: very cheap, usually accepts Mastercard/Visa. In Hong Kong, get an Octopus card (also works at shops).
  • Alipay / WeChat Pay: link a foreign card before arrival — most small vendors no longer accept cash.
  • September in Beijing: golden leaves begin. October: autumn peak with crisp, clear skies — ideal photography weather.
  • Some attractions need separate tickets: cable cars, Treasure Gallery (Forbidden City), Jingshan Park (10 CNY), glass skywalk (Zhangjiajie). Budget ~20–40 EUR extra per person per day for these.

Day 3
Thu, Sep 24
BeijingImperial core
🏯 Forbidden City · Tiananmen · Jingshan · Evening Qianmen
08:30
Relaxed breakfast at hotel, then taxi to Tiananmen Square (~¥30). The world's largest public square — walk across at your own pace, take photos of the Gate of Heavenly Peace. Completely flat, no queues.
09:15
Enter Forbidden City via Meridian Gate (pre-booked tickets). Pick up an audio guide (~40 CNY) — it means Dad can sit on a bench and listen rather than constantly walking. Follow the central axis at a leisurely pace: Hall of Supreme Harmony → Palace of Heavenly Purity → Imperial Garden. If feet get tired, find one of the many stone benches and sit — the architecture is worth studying slowly.
12:00
Midday rest — have lunch in the Forbidden City café near the Imperial Garden (surprisingly good) or exit and find a restaurant nearby. Take a full hour to sit and recover before the afternoon.
13:30
Exit north and enter Jingshan Park (10 CNY). The climb to Wanchun Pavilion is ~200 steps — take it slowly with rest stops, or enjoy the park gardens at the base if the climb isn't comfortable. The view from the top over the Forbidden City is the best in Beijing, but the park itself is beautiful at ground level too.
15:00
Tea break — find a teahouse near the park or in the Nanluoguxiang area. Sit for 30–45 minutes. This is not optional — feet need a rest before the evening.
16:30
Short taxi ride to Qianmen Street — a beautifully restored traditional shopping boulevard just south of Tiananmen. Flat, pedestrianised, easy to browse at any pace. Roast duck restaurants, traditional snack shops, and old-style storefronts.
18:30
Dinner at a Peking Duck restaurant on or near Qianmen — this is the essential Beijing meal. Sit down, order a whole duck, and enjoy properly. Da Dong or Quanjude are the famous names nearby.
The Forbidden City has many benches along the route — use them freely. The audio guide means Dad can absorb the history without needing to keep walking. Skip the internal galleries (Treasure Gallery, Clock Gallery) if energy is low — the main axis alone is extraordinary. Save Temple of Heaven for another city if time allows; don't try to do both in one day.

🏔 Mutianyu Great Wall · Summer Palace Lakeside Afternoon
08:30

Stroll the flat promenade along Kunming Lake, admire the Long Corridor (a 728m covered walkway with painted scenes), and enjoy the autumn light on the water. 17:00
Tea at a lakeside pavilion. Then taxi back to the hotel to freshen up before dinner.
19:00
Relaxed dinner near the hotel — Houhai Lake area has dozens of good restaurants with outdoor terraces and live music.
The cable car and toboggan at Mutianyu mean Dad gets the full Great Wall experience without any punishing stair climbs. The Summer Palace is ideal for older travellers — it's one of China's most beautiful gardens and entirely flat around the lake. Rickshaws are available for hire inside the grounds.

🌳 French Concession Walking Day · Tianzifang · Xintiandi
08:30
Start at Fuxing Park — a French-style garden where locals do tai chi, ballroom dancing, and card games each morning. Feels like stepping into old Shanghai.

16:00
Shaoxing Road / Sinan Mansions area — the most artistic block in the Concession, home to publishing houses, galleries, and specialty coffee shops.
18:00
Pre-dinner cocktail at a French Concession rooftop bar. Then dinner at a local Shanghainese restaurant: hongshao rou (red-braised pork), drunken chicken, scallion oil noodles.
20:30
Optional: live jazz in the French Concession — the area has several intimate jazz clubs with nightly performances, a genuine Shanghai tradition since the 1930s.
The FFC is best explored on foot or by bike. September evenings are warm and pleasant. Take the metro home — Line 1 runs through the area (South Shaanxi Road station).

ZhangjiajieTianmen Mountain 🔴 Golden Week Day 1
🌉 Tianmen Mountain — Cable Car · Glass Skywalk · Heaven's Gate Cave
06:30
Be at the Tianmen Mountain cable car station before it opens — this is the world's longest passenger cable car (7.4km, ~30 min ride). Golden Week Day 1: queues start forming before 7am. Book tickets online the night before.
07:30
Ride up for sunrise views over the sea of forested peaks. Take the cable car to Cloud Terrace Square at the summit plateau (1,518m).
09:00
Walk the Coiling Dragon Cliff Glass Skywalk — a glass walkway cantilevered over a sheer 1,400m drop. Terrifying, brilliant. Pre-book the skywalk time slot to avoid long waits.
11:00
Hike or take the shuttle bus to Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate) — a natural arch 131m high punched through the cliff face. The view from inside looking out is stunning. Walk the 999 steps up through the cave.
13:00
Lunch at summit.
14:30
Descend on the Tianmen Mountain Road via bus — 99 hairpin bends carved into the cliff. Then walk back through the forest. Or take the cable car back down.
17:00
Back in Zhangjiajie city. Explore the central market area — local food stalls and Tujia ethnic handicrafts.
Golden Week Day 1 — all of Tianmen Mountain will be packed. Go at 6:30am or accept 2-hour queues. Pre-book cable car and skywalk tickets online. Early risers get misty morning views before crowds arrive.

Strongly recommended addition — among the most stunning landscapes in China, especially in October harvest season.

13:00

October is the absolute best time to visit Longji — golden rice harvest makes the terraces glow. If you can only do one day trip from Guilin, make it this one. The terraces carved into mountains over 600 years ago are an extraordinary feat of engineering.

Sun, Oct 4
Guilin → YangshuoLi River Cruise
⛵ Li River Morning Cruise · Yangshuo Afternoon
08:00
Transfer to Guilin Mopanshan Pier. Board the Li River cruise (3-star government-operated boats recommended as more reliable — depart ~09:00, cruise ticket ~25 EUR/¥200). The 4-hour journey downstream is considered one of the world's great scenic boat rides.
09:00–13:00
Float past 83km of karst peaks, bamboo groves, fishing villages, and water buffalo in the rice paddies. Key highlights: Nine Horse Fresco Hill, Yellow Cloth Shoal, Xingping Ancient Town (the scene on the 20 RMB banknote). Bring a good camera.
13:00
Arrive Yangshuo. Check in at Shengjin Hotel (Lao Street, Xingping — a quieter, more scenic location than central Yangshuo town).
14:30
Explore Xingping Ancient Town on foot — beautifully preserved fishing village with old stone lanes, teahouses, and the exact Li River view printed on the 20-yuan note. Find the viewpoint and take the photo.
16:00
Rent a bicycle (¥30–50/day everywhere) and cycle the Yulong River scenic route — quiet roads through rice paddies and karst peaks, far less busy than central Yangshuo.
18:30
Head into Yangshuo West Street for dinner — the main tourist drag, but good food. Try Yangshuo Beer Fish (local river carp cooked with local beer) and rice noodles with duck. Live music in several bars.
The 20 RMB banknote view near Xingping is one of the classic China photo moments. The Yulong River (Little Li River) cycling route is quieter than the main town and more scenic — highly recommended over West Street sightseeing.

Shenzhen → MacauOvernight in old town
🎰 Macau Old Town · Ruins of St. Paul's · Senado Square
09:00
Travel from Shenzhen to Macau. Best route: take metro to Shekou Ferry Terminal (Line 2, Shekou Cruise Centre station) and catch a TurboJet or Cotai Jet ferry direct to Macau (~1h, ~¥200–280). Alternatively ferry from HK (~75 min).
11:00
Arrive Macau. Walk or taxi to Senado Square (Largo do Senado) — the historic centre, paved with black-and-white Portuguese wave tiles. World Heritage Site. Lined with pastel colonial buildings, egg tart bakeries, and almond cookie shops.
12:00
Ruins of St. Paul's — the iconic 17th-century Jesuit church facade. Climb behind it for a panoramic view across the old town. Fascinating contrast: a European baroque church in South China.
13:00
Lunch: egg tarts and pork chop buns (Macanese staples) in Senado Square area. Lord Stow's or Margaret's Café are the best egg tart spots.
14:30
Walk up to Fortaleza do Monte (free) — a 17th-century Portuguese fortress with cannon views over the old town. Attached Macau Museum (¥15) traces the territory's remarkable history.
16:00
A-Ma Temple — one of Macau's oldest temples, a Taoist shrine from 1488. The incense coils and red lanterns create a powerful atmosphere. Very different from mainland Chinese temples.
17:30
Check in at Pensão Família (old town area — ideal base for the heritage streets).
19:00
Evening stroll through the old town lit up at night. Try Macanese-Portuguese dinner: African chicken, Portuguese bacalhau, minchi (minced meat with potato). Then peek at the exterior of a casino if curious — the Venetian is the largest building in Asia.
Macau's UNESCO-listed historic centre is surprisingly compact and walkable in an afternoon. The Portuguese egg tarts are a must — buy them warm from a bakery in Senado Square. Keep spending modest; even the free casino floors are a spectacle.
